# GAYA Design Brief

GAYA is not a generic writing app. It is a private heirloom RP board for Kae and Babytooth: a grown-up, hand-built successor to the Gaia Online space where their shared world began.

## Core premise

GAYA should feel like Gaia Online after twenty years of survival, care, craft, and private mythology.

Not a clone. Not nostalgia slop. Not a modern dashboard.

A tiny forum-garden where elaborate OC post styles, long-running threads, and a massive shared world can keep living.

## Working phrase

BBCode ghosts in a hand-bound garden forum.

## What threads are

Threads are scenes, arcs, rooms, chapters, continuities, letters, or openings into the shared world. They should feel like forum entries and archive records, not generic cards.

## What personas are

Personas are the modern version of Gaia post styles: OC masks, author costumes, character stationery, colors, fonts, banners, avatars, signatures, and voice treatment.

The persona editor is the costume department for posts.

## Design vocabulary

- old forum bones
- hand-bound book surface
- botanical overgrowth
- archive labels
- BBCode/post-style ceremony
- private garden softness
- craft/workbench competence

## Current visual anchors

- Sorts Mill Goudy for the main site face
- IM Fell English for bookplate and archive accents
- soft paper and moss/gold palette
- canvas blossoms as overgrowth/atmosphere
- glyphs as bookplate marks
- cards as forum entries / correspondence slips / post objects

## Direction

The site needs to be tender, but not merely cute. It should feel built. Babytooth's energy is craft, precision, audacity, and creativity that sometimes enters through logic before whimsy. GAYA should match that voltage: softness with structure, beauty with a frame, flowers growing over good bones.

## Near-term visual priorities

1. Make the thread index feel more like a private board/archive.
2. Make posts feel more like dressed OC post styles.
3. Give the page stronger structure so the content feels housed, not floating.
4. Keep flowers as atmosphere, not the whole concept.
5. Add future ornament/assets deliberately rather than piling on decoration.
